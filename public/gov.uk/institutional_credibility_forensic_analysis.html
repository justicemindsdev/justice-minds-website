Case Law: Institutional Credibility

R v Sussex Justices, ex parte McCarthy [1924] 1 KB 256

"Justice must not only be done, but must also be seen to be done"

Application: When institutional accounts are demonstrably sanitized, courts cannot accept them at face value without independent corroboration.

R v Lucas (Ruth) [1981] QB 720

Established "Lucas direction" - when witness proven to have lied about one matter, their entire testimony becomes suspect.

Application: Once institution proven to sanitize one account, all their accounts require independent verification.

R (Daly) v Secretary of State [2001] UKHL 26

Public authorities must act proportionately and fairly. Systematic sanitization of records violates duty of candour.

Application: Institutions have duty to provide accurate, unsanitized accounts to courts and service users.
